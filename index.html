About

I am a master's student in National Economics at Nanjing University, under the supervision of Prof. Haitao Huang. My research focuses on applying large language models and textual analysis to finance, with particular interest in measuring managerial expectations and belief heterogeneity from corporate disclosures.


Research Interests: Textual Analysis in Finance, Managerial Expectation, Empirical Asset Pricing

Working Papers

  • Decoding Managerial Beliefs from Corporate Disclosure: Measuring Expectation Divergence with LLMs
    with Zhenyu Gao and Haitao Huang (April 2026)

    Present at: CICF 2026 (Scheduled); Greater China Area Finance Conference 2026 (Scheduled); MRS International Risk Conference 2026 (Scheduled); Annual Graduate Economics Academic Conference 2026 (Second Best Paper Award); Tongji University 2026; Nanjing University 2025

    Metric Construction: Constructed a novel "Firm Expectation Divergence" index by leveraging Large Language Models to extract structured beliefs from 41,000+ MD&A texts (2012-2024), effectively distinguishing rational expectations from emotional sentiment.

    Empirical Findings: Documented a "Dual Anomaly": high divergence predicts deteriorating future fundamentals (ROA/ROE) due to managerial information processing defects, yet positively predicts risk-adjusted stock returns (Alpha).

    Mechanism: Validated the "Risk Premium Channel," showing that divergence proxies for idiosyncratic risk and information uncertainty, compelling rational investors to demand higher compensation.

Work in Progress

  • Policy in the Eye of the Analyst
    Research Assistant, supervised by Prof. Pengfei Sui (CUHK-Shenzhen)  (Mar 2026 – Jun 2026)

    Data Construction: Analyst report database: ~267,731 reports from 1,607 institutions covering 2017-2025. Policy database from PKULAW: ~970,000 policies covering 2000-2025.

    Disagreement Measure: Using LLMs, we decompose analysts' policy reasoning in industry reports into distinct dimensions and compute the heterogeneous belief (disagreement) that exists among analysts covering the same industry after the same policy shock.
