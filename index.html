About

I am a master's student in National Economics at Nanjing University, under the supervision of Prof. Haitao Huang. My research focuses on applying large language models and textual analysis to finance, with particular interest in measuring managerial expectations and belief heterogeneity from corporate disclosures.


Research Interests: Textual Analysis in Finance, Managerial Expectation, Empirical Asset Pricing

Working Papers

  • Decoding Managerial Beliefs from Corporate Disclosure: Measuring Expectation Divergence with LLMs
    with Zhenyu Gao and Haitao Huang (June 2026) [PDF]
    Present at: CICF 2026 (Scheduled); Greater China Area Finance Conference 2026 (Scheduled); MRS International Risk Conference 2026 (Scheduled); Annual Graduate Economics Academic Conference 2026 (Second Best Paper Award); Tongji University 2026; Nanjing University 2025

    When a firm's management holds a macroeconomic outlook that diverges from its industry peers, does the deviation signal superior private information or cognitive error? We measure managerial macroeconomic expectations directly, applying a Large Language Model to the forward-looking disclosures of Chinese A-share listed firms from 2012 to 2024, and define expectation divergence as a firm's deviation from its industry-year consensus. Divergence predicts both deteriorating future operating performance and higher future risk-adjusted returns, and both effects load on the pessimistic component rather than the optimistic one. The evidence is consistent with a mechanism in which managers over-extrapolate salient recent firm-specific experience to the common macro environment. On the real side, the distortion is partially self-fulfilling, as managers retrench investment, R&D, and hiring beyond what realized fundamentals warrant, producing a profitability shortfall that proves transitory. On the pricing side, the over-pessimism is impounded into prices, generating a positive return that attenuates with analyst coverage, institutional ownership, size, and liquidity, consistent with mispricing sustained by limits to arbitrage rather than an unconditional risk premium.
